ESP8266 Digital Compass with Web UI & Wi-Fi Configurator
This project turns an ESP8266 and a QMC5883L magnetometer into a smart digital compass. It features a dual-mode Wi-Fi logic: if it cannot find a saved network, it creates its own Access Point (Hotspot) allowing you to configure Wi-Fi credentials via a web browser. Once configured, it connects to your local network and serves a real-time, dark-themed compass dashboard.

ðŸ“– How It Works
Phase 1: Configuration (First Time Use)
1.	Power on the device.
2.	Search for Wi-Fi on your phone and connect to "Compass_Setup_AP".
3.	Open 192.168.4.1 in your browser.
4.	Enter your Home Wi-Fi SSID and Password and click Save.
5.	The device will save these to EEPROM and reboot.
Phase 2: Operation
1.	The ESP8266 connects to your home Wi-Fi.
2.	Check the Serial Monitor to find the new IP address assigned by your router.
3.	Open that IP address in any browser on your network.
4.	Calibration: Rotate the sensor in a "figure 8" motion for the first 30 seconds (indicated on screen).
5.	View your live heading and cardinal direction (N, NE, E, etc.).
